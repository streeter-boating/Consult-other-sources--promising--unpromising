In the small, dark, theatre a rock trio called Weep Wave blasted their Idles-esque guitar rock over reverb drenched vocals. Songs quickly four-counted into the next, which gave a seamless feeling to their performance. The lead singer played a red SG, and would open his eyes wide and shout into the mic. One of our favorite performances from the day. Finally we saw rapper Mavi perform a passionate set on the Vera stage. His raps are dense and unique. I feel him as a more melodic Earl Sweatshirt, with beats that bounce a little more. He was a connected performer who loved to get the crowd involved. At the end of his set he waded into the crowd and asked us to "gimme a hug, Seattle".
